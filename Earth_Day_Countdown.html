About Earth Day

Earth Day, celebrated every year on April 22nd, marks a global commitment to environmental protection and sustainability. It's a day when individuals, communities, and organizations around the world engage in activities to promote ecological awareness and take action towards preserving our planet.
Since its inception in 1970, Earth Day has grown into a worldwide movement, emphasizing the urgent need to address environmental issues such as climate change, pollution, and wildlife conservation.

Carbon Footprint
The total amount of greenhouse gases produced to directly and indirectly support human activities, typically expressed in equivalent tons of carbon dioxide (CO2).
This includes emissions from burning fossil fuels for electricity, heat, and transportation, as well as emissions from industries, agriculture, and deforestation. Reducing our carbon footprint is crucial in combating climate change, and it can be achieved through energy efficiency, adopting renewable energy sources, and changing consumption habits.
